## ğŸŒ¡ï¸ Why Does This Work?

**Confusion doesn't maintain itself.**

If nobody benefits from keeping you confused, the confusion clears up on its own. You ask questions, you get answers, things make sense eventually. Information wants to flow.

But sometimes confusion *doesn't* clear. You ask questions and end up more confused. Or the questions themselves start feeling dangerous.

That's not natural. That takes effort. Someone is *maintaining* that fog.

And maintenance leaves fingerprints.

> Sustained confusion in a situation where clarity should be easy means someone is actively preventing clarity. The persistence is the evidence.

You don't need to prove intent. You don't need to read minds. Just notice the fog isn't clearing when it should, and ask: **who benefits from this staying foggy?**

## ğŸ› ï¸ What To Do When You See It

The framework aims for **æ­¸é›¶**â€”return to zero. Make M go to zero.

You have three levers:

**Clear the fog (lower A)**
> "I notice I'm getting confused every time I ask about X."

Sometimes just naming it dissolves it.

**Lower the cost (reduce ARC)**

Find ways to ask questions that don't feel dangerous. Find others with the same questions. Get financially or emotionally independent. Practice small questions until big ones feel possible.

**Turn on the lights (raise V)**

The framework doesn't tell you which lever to pull. It shows you the levers exist.
