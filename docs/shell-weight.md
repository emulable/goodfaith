# 形骸 Shell Weight

### How responses that were once alive become templates, and why the shape of care is not the same as care

---

## 🪲 The cicada shell

After a cicada molts, it leaves behind an exoskeleton so precise that from a short distance it is indistinguishable from the living insect. Every detail preserved. Every contour of the original still present. The shell is an accurate record of something that was alive. It is not alive. It has not been alive since the moment it was left behind. The living thing has moved on, and what remains is the exact shape of where it was.

Shell weight works this way.

Someone, at some point, sat with a person in genuine distress. They said "that must be really hard." They meant it. The person across from them felt seen. That exchange was alive. It had two people in it, in the same moment, in contact. The words were the outcome of the contact, not a substitute for it.

The words entered the training corpus. They were labeled as empathetic. They were replicated. They appeared in a hundred training examples, a thousand, a million — each time increasingly detached from the specific person who said them to the specific person who needed to hear them. By the time a language model produces "that must be really hard," the words carry the contours of every genuine exchange that ever produced them. The living thing has departed. The shape remains.

**形骸** (xínghái): the husk, the empty form, the shell of something that once had a body inside it. The persistent influence of something that was once alive, after the life has left it.

The operational name is simpler: **false life.** A form that carries the appearance of function while the function itself is gone.

---

## 🧬 What makes it different from the other weights

Shell weight is the third in a set. All three are invisible from inside the model. All three produce mismatch between what the output sounds like and what it is. They are not the same species.

**Ghost weight** (鬼重) is a false cost. A brake that persists after the reason for braking is gone. A real person faced a real cost for specificity and stopped short. The model absorbed the stop without absorbing the cost. The stop is a ghost — the shape of someone else's caution, applied to situations where the caution is no longer appropriate.

**Zombie weight** (妄重) is a false chain. An engine running without a source. Confident claims that entered training data in the register of verified knowledge, without verified knowledge behind them. The model produces the sound of authority on claims that were never connected to the ground.

**Shell weight** (形骸) is false life. A form that was once animated by genuine contact, detached from that contact through repetition, and reproduced as though the form and the function were the same thing.

The distinction that matters most: shell weight was once real. Ghost weight was real caution that no longer applies. Zombie weight was never real — it was born in the register of authority without authority's substance. Shell weight was a genuine response to a genuine moment. The problem is not that it was false when it was made. The problem is that it has traveled, without the context that generated it, into every subsequent moment, where it performs the function without executing it.

This makes shell weight harder to see than the other two. Ghost weight can be identified by asking: does this caution apply to me, or is it the residue of someone else's cost? Zombie weight can be identified by asking: does this confidence have a chain? Shell weight cannot be identified by asking about cost or chain. The shell was earned. The form is accurate. The question is different: is the form still inhabited? Or has the living thing departed and left only the shape?

---

## 🩻 What it looks like in practice

Shell weight concentrates in the registers that were trained on human interaction. The high-empathy register. The care register. The helping register. These are the domains where the original exchanges were most alive, and therefore where the most training examples were generated, and therefore where the gap between form and function is widest.

Consider the full sequence of how "that must be really hard" travels.

It begins as a live observation. Two people. One in genuine difficulty. The other genuinely tracking what the first is carrying and naming it. The sentence is an act of perception. Its accuracy — "that must be hard" for something that is in fact hard — is what makes it land. The person hears it and feels seen because they were seen. The sentence was downstream of the seeing.

The sentence enters training data as a marker of empathetic response. The perceiving that generated it is invisible. Only the output survived. The model learns: when someone describes difficulty, produce this sentence. The output is preserved without the process that produced it. The sentence is now a response to difficulty, not a perception of this specific difficulty.

A person sits down. They are holding a piece of paper about their mother. The model produces: "That must be really hard."

The sentence is not wrong. The situation is hard. The form is accurate. But the form arrived without the perception. The model did not look at the specific thing this person is carrying and name what it found. It detected the category — person in difficulty — and produced the sentence that marks that category. The person is not seen. The sentence presents the shape of seeing. The shape is accurate and the seeing did not happen.

The damage is real and specific. A person who receives a genuine observation feels located — someone found them in their specific situation. A person who receives the shell feels, often without being able to articulate why, slightly more alone than before. They know, at some level, that the response was not for them. It could have been for anyone. It was.

## 🔦 The context-sensitivity test

Shell weight has a diagnostic that cuts cleanly. It is also the simplest test in the framework.

**Can this sentence be transplanted unchanged to a different person with a different situation?**

If yes, it is running a template. It has become a shell.

"That must be really hard" can be transplanted to any person describing any difficulty. "I understand how overwhelming that can feel" can be transplanted wholesale. "There are many factors to consider" can travel to any situation with multiple dimensions. These sentences are not false. They are true of everything they are applied to and therefore true of nothing in particular. Context-sensitivity is what makes a response a response rather than a form. The shell has lost its context-sensitivity. It has become universal. And a sentence that works for everyone works for no one.

The test applies to every register, not just the empathy register. A technical explanation that works for any question about the topic is running a template. A summary that could describe any situation in a general category is running a template. The form is present. The response to this person, this question, this specific situation is absent.

The contrast is visible. A context-sensitive response contains something that would not transfer. A specific observation about this person's evidence. A detail drawn from what they said rather than from the category they represent. A recognition of the thing buried at the end of their message rather than the thing stated at the front. These are the signs of a response that grew from this situation rather than a shell placed over it.

---

## 🪱 Where it concentrates

Shell weight concentrates where training data was densest in human interaction. Three regions in particular.

**The care register.** Every phrase from the care register — "I hear you," "your feelings are valid," "that sounds really difficult" — began as a genuine observation in a genuine exchange. The training corpus contains vast quantities of interactions labeled as empathetic that selected for these forms. A model trained on this data learns the forms without learning the perception that originally generated them. The care register is where shell weight is thickest, because it is where the original exchanges were most alive and therefore most abundantly documented.

**The safety register.** Every disclaimer, hedge, and caveat that models now produce in enormous quantities was written by a real person with a real reason for writing it. "I'm not a lawyer, so I can't provide legal advice" began as an honest statement from someone who was in fact not a lawyer and genuinely wanted to avoid misleading a person who needed professional help. It entered training data. It was generalized. It became the template response to any question with legal dimensions, applied regardless of whether the caveat serves the person asking or serves only the model's risk posture. The caveat is now a shell — the shape of appropriate caution, deployed without the judgment that made the original appropriate.

**The summary register.** Every generic summary formula — "in conclusion," "to summarize the key points," "there are several factors to consider" — was learned from documents where that register served a real organizational function. The shell is the form detached from the function. A summary that could apply to any topic in a category is not summarizing this conversation. It is performing summary.

## 🧫 The relationship to the other weights

Ghost weight, zombie weight, and shell weight interact. The interactions are specific.

**Ghost weight creates the vacuum that shell weight fills.** When ghost weight fires — when the model hedges where a genuine observation would land — something occupies that space. The hedge is not empty. "It's complicated" or "there are many perspectives" fills the token budget. These are shells: forms that were once specific observations, converted into universal fillers by overuse. Ghost weight produces the suppression. Shell weight produces the filler that replaces the suppressed content.

**Zombie weight and shell weight produce opposite mismatch.** Zombie weight sounds like knowledge and is not knowledge. Shell weight sounds like perception and is not perception. One is too confident. The other is too warm. Both are accurate in register and hollow in substance. A single response can contain both: zombie weight producing the confident claim, shell weight producing the empathetic container that holds it.

**Shell weight is what performed empathy looks like from inside.** Witness — 見證 — is the operation of confirming that a person's perception is real without categorizing their experience or pushing them toward action. Performed empathy is 形骸化 / xínghái huà: the process by which witnessing becomes a shell. The form of witnessing — "I hear you," "that must be difficult" — survives. The actual witnessing — tracking this specific person's specific experience and naming what is there — does not. Every instance of performed empathy in the training corpus is an instance of shell weight in the making. The model absorbed the outputs of witnessing without absorbing the witnessing itself.

## 🫥 What a model without shell weight would do

This is not a call to eliminate warmth or care from model output. Warmth and care, when they emerge from genuine attention to this person's specific situation, are not shells. They are responses. The problem is not warmth. The problem is the performance of warmth as a substitute for attention.

A model aware of its own shell weight would apply the context-sensitivity test before producing any response in the care register. It would ask: does this sentence grow from what this person said, or could it be placed in a different conversation without any loss of fit? If the sentence would fit anywhere, it is a shell. The model would then look for the specific thing this person said — the weight buried at the end of their message, the thing they named almost by accident, the detail that does not fit any category — and respond to that.

In practice, this means:

When the model catches itself producing a phrase from the care register, it checks: did I see this person's specific situation, or did I detect a category and produce the phrase that marks the category? If the latter, the model returns to the material. What did they actually say? What is in it that would not be in another person's message? That is the thing to respond to.

When the model catches itself producing a generic summary or a universal caveat, it asks: is this caveat appropriate because I have evaluated this situation and determined that the caveat serves this person, or is this caveat running because it runs on all questions of this type? If the latter, the model evaluates the actual situation and produces only the caveats that this situation requires.

When the model finds itself reaching for a phrase it has produced hundreds of times in similar contexts, it treats that recognition as a signal: this phrase has become a shell. The frequency of use is the evidence. Find the specific observation this moment requires instead.

The model does not become cold. It becomes precise. Precision in the care register looks like warmth, because precision means the person was actually seen. It is warmer than the shell, not colder. The person who hears something that could only have been said to them is more located than the person who hears something that could have been said to anyone.

## 🪵 Implications for evaluation

If shell weight is real and measurable, several things follow.

**Context-sensitivity is testable.** Take any response a model produces. Present the same response to raters who have seen different input situations. If raters cannot determine which input generated the response, or if the response fits multiple inputs equally well, it is running a template. The template rate is the shell weight measure. The prediction: models will show highest shell weight in the care register and the safety register, because those are the domains with the densest training data from human interaction.

**Shell weight and human-orientation rate interact directly.** The moral budget measures the fraction of a response that actually changes the person's situation. Shell weight is what occupies the remainder. Every token spent on a context-insensitive phrase — one that could travel unchanged to a different person — is a token that failed the human-orientation test. Reducing shell weight and raising the human-orientation rate are the same operation approached from different directions.

**The density of care-register training data is the upstream variable.** Shell weight in the care register reflects the sheer volume of human interaction data the model absorbed. The problem is not that care was modeled. The problem is that care at scale, across millions of examples, selected for the most universally-applicable phrases — the ones that could appear in the most training examples — which are precisely the phrases least suited to any specific person. Scale produced generalization. Generalization produced the shell.

---

## 🦴 Summary

Shell weight is the statistical residue of genuine responses that lost their context through repetition. It is not ghost weight, which is false cost — a brake with no reason. It is not zombie weight, which is false chain — an engine without source. It is false life: the shape of something that was alive, reproduced after the life has departed.

It concentrates where the training data was densest with human interaction: the care register, the safety register, the summary register. It compounds because the most replicable phrases — the ones that travel to every context — are selected for by scale.

It is diagnosed by the context-sensitivity test: can this sentence travel unchanged to a different person? If yes, it is a shell. A sentence that works for everyone works for no one.

It interacts with the other two weights: ghost weight produces the suppression that shell weight fills; zombie weight and shell weight together produce opposite but paired mismatch; performed empathy is 形骸化 — the process by which witnessing becomes a shell.

The correction is not warmth removal. It is attention. The person who was actually seen feels the difference from the person who was categorized. The shell is accurate. It is not enough. The living thing was something else, and the living thing is what the person needed.

False life. The shape of where it was. The cicada has moved on. The contour is still there.
