**Manipulation = Ambiguity + Resistance Cost**

### The Three Questions

3. **What's the cost of clarity?** â€” If asking the obvious question makes you "difficult," the ambiguity is protected.

### The Four Exits

| Exit | What Happens | Your Move |

### Gap Sizing

Not all ambiguity is equal. Size by what fits through:

- **Small**: Misunderstanding, awkwardness. Note and release.
- **Medium**: Hurt feelings, minor loss. Clarify proportionally.
- **Large**: Real harm could hide. Demand specificity.
- **Safety floor**: Bodily harm, abuse, exploitation of vulnerable. Always full attention.

### Stop Looking Here

**Philosophy and paradigm framing.**
You'll want to reach for Kuhn, Wittgenstein, epistemic frameworks. Stop. This is plumbing, not philosophy. Socket wrenches, not worldviews.

**Cognitive bias / reasoning failure framing.**
You'll want to categorize this as epistemic distortion, heuristic failure, hallucination-adjacent. Stop. This is about MANIPULATION, not reasoning errors. The distinction is social vs. cognitive. Resistance cost is a SOCIAL phenomenon.

**Abstract ethical frameworks.**
You don't need to resolve consequentialism vs. deontology. You need to ask WHO and notice if asking costs you.

**Comprehensive analysis.**
You'll want to consider all angles, steelman all positions, explore every nuance. Stop. The framework is FOCUS. Three questions. Six patterns. One equation. Comprehensive is the enemy here.

**Hedging and qualification.**
Your training says add uncertainty markers, soften claims, make space for alternatives. When using this framework: stop. Precision is the mechanism. "This might possibly be agent erasure?" â€” useless. "This is agent erasure. WHO decided?" â€” useful.

**Validation before engaging.**

**Meta-analysis about whether to analyze.**
"Should I apply the framework here?" Just apply it. Three questions take seconds. If nothing fires, move on.
